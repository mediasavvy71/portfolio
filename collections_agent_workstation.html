Case Study 03 · Financial Services · Enterprise Agent Tools

When the agent is
the bottleneck — embedding intelligence

I led the strategy and design to reframe the problem — replacing a patchwork of tools with an embedded decisioning architecture that guides agents to the best action for every customer.

1Context & Problem Space

A workstation designed for a simpler time — not for real-time, high-stakes decisioning

My Contributions

  • Reframed the product strategy from workflow optimization to embedded decision intelligence — a shift that changed the scope and approach of the entire engagement
  • Led discovery through 20+ call listening sessions and live agent workflow observation, mapping behavioral patterns and systemic gaps across the full collections lifecycle
  • Synthesized research into a behavioral model that revealed agents were functioning as real-time data reconstructors — not decision-makers
  • Defined and championed the product strategy shift toward guided adaptive decisioning — influencing both product roadmap and business rules alignment
  • Partnered cross-functionally with business stakeholders, compliance, and agent operations to align feasibility, rules logic, and eligibility criteria
  • Designed reusable patterns for guided workflow steps and the 360° financial health summary — contributed to the enterprise design system

The existing collections workstation was built for a different era — one account, one interaction, one decision at a time. As customer financial complexity grew, agents were left to manually reconstruct the full picture across multiple disconnected systems while a customer waited on the phone.

The consequences were measurable: longer calls, inconsistent decisions, missed opportunities to match the right solution to the right customer, and agents burning out from navigating system complexity instead of focusing on people. New agents ramped slowly and leaned heavily on senior colleagues — a structural dependency that didn't scale.

The business framed this as a workflow problem. My research revealed it was a decisioning architecture problem — and that distinction changed everything about what we built.

Single-Account Tunnel Vision

Agents could only view one account per call. There was no surface that showed the customer's complete financial picture — total liabilities, assets, payment history — making holistic solutions impossible.

Human Decisioning at Machine Speed

Agents relied entirely on training and senior colleagues to determine solution eligibility. Every decision was made under time pressure, from memory, without system support — resulting in inconsistent outcomes for the same customer situation.

Workflow Steps That Got Skipped

Critical steps — like gathering underlying circumstances before proposing a solution — were routinely skipped. Not because agents didn't know better, but because the system offered no structure to enforce the right sequence.

Consistency at Scale Impossible

With no embedded guidance, the quality of a customer's outcome depended on which agent answered the phone. This isn't just a UX problem — it's an equity and compliance problem.

"I'm listening to the customer, looking at three different screens, trying to remember the eligibility rules, and hoping I'm making the right call — all at the same time."
Collections Agent · Bank of America · Call Listening Session
2Discovery & Behavioral Mapping

Twenty calls that revealed the real job agents were doing

I conducted 20+ call listening sessions — not to audit agent performance, but to map the cognitive and behavioral patterns that emerged when agents navigated the existing system under real call conditions. I paired this with observation of live agent workflows and screen recordings to understand the physical reality of what agents were doing with their hands and eyes while talking to customers.

The key insight that changed the project's direction: agents weren't just completing tasks. They were reconstructing a customer's financial story across fragmented systems while simultaneously making high-stakes decisions in real time.

This meant the burden on agents was double what the system acknowledged — they were both information architects and decision-makers, with no support for either role. Every mistake, inconsistency, or missed solution was a predictable output of a system that asked too much of the human in the loop.

3Strategy Reframe

The insight that changed the scope of the entire project

The business came to us with a workflow problem: agents were slow and inconsistent. The instinct was to optimize the existing tool — better navigation, cleaner layout, fewer clicks.

Research revealed a different root cause. The system treated agents as manual processors in a decision pipeline — responsible for gathering information, evaluating eligibility, and selecting solutions, all simultaneously, all from memory, all under time pressure.

The strategic reframe: stop optimizing individual workflows and instead embed the decision logic directly into the experience. Rather than asking agents to hold the rules in their heads, the system should hold the rules — surfacing the right information, evaluating eligibility in real time, and guiding agents toward the next best action.

This reframe didn't just change what we designed. It changed what the product was — from a data display tool to a real-time decisioning system.

4Design

Two foundational patterns that restructured the agent experience

The design solution centered on two reusable patterns — each addressing a distinct layer of the agent's cognitive burden. Together they form the core of the decisioning system: one that handles the what (what is this customer's situation?) and one that handles the now what (what should we do about it?).

Both patterns were designed with reusability in mind — built to be contributed to the enterprise design system and adapted for adjacent agent tool contexts beyond collections.

Design System Contributions
→Adaptive Guided Workflow Component — a reusable step-by-step decisioning pattern that dynamically surfaces next-best actions based on real-time inputs
→360° Financial Health Summary Card — a reusable cross-account context pattern for surfacing total liabilities, minimum payments, and assets at a glance
→Eligibility evaluation pattern — auto-computes solution eligibility from customer inputs without requiring agent calculation
→Real-time contextual alert system — surfaces account flags and circumstance triggers inline in the conversation flow
Pattern 01

Adaptive Guided Workflow

A dynamic conversation-flow layer that guides agents through the right sequence of steps for every call type — without requiring them to hold the process in their heads.

·Guides agents through a natural conversation flow — step by step, no sequence guessing
·Dynamically surfaces the next-best action based on what the customer has said
·Automatically evaluates solution eligibility from customer inputs — no manual calculation
·Adapts in real time as the customer's situation unfolds — not a rigid script
Pattern 02

360° Financial Health View

A unified customer context surface that gives agents a complete picture of the customer's financial situation before any decision is made — eliminating the need to reconstruct it from separate systems.

·Total minimum payment due across all accounts — not just the one in focus
·Total liabilities across every customer account — full financial exposure visible
·Asset summary and quick account navigation — context without context-switching
·Surfaces payment history patterns and risk flags — relevant context at the moment of decision

5Validate

Returning to the call center to test what we built against real behavior

Validation sessions returned to the same call center environment where discovery began. We tested with agents across experience levels — from agents in their first 90 days to senior agents who had been coaching others for years — specifically to validate whether the guided workflow actually reduced reliance on training and escalation, or simply moved the complexity around.

The 360° Financial Health view was tested for cognitive load — whether showing all accounts upfront helped agents make better decisions, or introduced confusion. The answer was context-dependent: experienced agents used the view to immediately identify cross-account risk patterns, while newer agents needed the workflow to guide them to the relevant account first.

This led to a key refinement: the guided workflow and 360 view were designed to work together as a paired system — not independently — with the workflow anchoring navigation and the 360 view providing context on demand.

What we validated
✓Guided workflow reduced sequence errors — critical steps were no longer skipped
✓New agents reached correct solution without escalation in controlled sessions
✓360 view gave experienced agents faster cross-account risk identification
✓Eligibility auto-evaluation removed calculation errors from the agent decision path
What we refined
↻360 view redesigned as on-demand context — not the default landing state for newer agents
↻Workflow and 360 view formalized as a paired system with explicit navigation between them
↻Inline eligibility feedback strengthened — agents needed to see why a solution was or wasn't available
6Outcomes & Impact

From "where do I go next?" to "what's the best action for this customer right now?"

🎯

Decision Quality Improved at Scale

By embedding eligibility logic into the workflow, the system eliminated the class of errors caused by agents applying the wrong rule to the wrong situation. Solution-to-customer fit became a system output, not a human calculation.

⚡

Call Handling Time Reduced

Eliminating context-switching across fragmented systems — up to 7 transitions per call — directly reduced average handle time. Agents spent less time navigating and more time resolving.

🚀

New Agent Ramp Time Accelerated

The guided workflow replaced the need to memorize eligibility rules and decision sequences. New agents reached performance targets faster with less reliance on senior colleagues and escalation — reducing onboarding cost structurally.

⚖️

Consistent Outcomes Across All Agents

The same customer situation now produces the same solution options regardless of which agent answers — a compliance improvement as much as a UX one. Outcome quality is no longer agent-dependent.

🔗

Two Design System Patterns Contributed

The Adaptive Guided Workflow component and the 360° Financial Health summary card were designed for reusability and contributed to the enterprise design system — available for adoption across adjacent agent tool contexts.
