1Context & Problem Space

A suite of products growing in silos, losing ground to modern competitors

My Contributions

  • Facilitated and participated in stakeholder and user interviews across buyer, approver, and finance roles
  • Synthesized qualitative research into personas and journey maps
  • Led creation of user flows, storyboards, wireframes, and high-fidelity mockups
  • Partnered with the Fireball Design System team to drive unified visual and interaction standards
  • Influenced product roadmap priorities through design strategy and executive communication

Individual vs. Integrated

Products were acquired and developed by separate teams, each with distinct workflows and visual languages. Users switching between tools experienced constant context disruption.

Confusing Workflows

Features were built without understanding how users actually work. Repetitive, deep-click task flows generated substantial negative feedback from clients and internal product teams.

Inconsistent Aesthetics

Healthcare organizations didn't perceive the suite as a unified platform. Competing visual styles undermined trust and made demos feel disjointed to prospective buyers.

"Whenever anyone demos our products it takes…like…four clicks to get somewhere to actually do something… It is embarrassing… nothing looks the same… we are getting our lunch handed to us by our competitors."
VP of Supply Chain Management
2Research & Discovery

Listening to the full procurement ecosystem—from solo practitioners to enterprise teams

We conducted contextual interviews and workflow observations across healthcare organizations of varying sizes. A key insight emerged early: org size fundamentally shapes how users navigate the procure-to-pay journey—a finding that directly informed our persona and workflow architecture.

Stakeholder Interviews Contextual Observation Use Case Matrix Workflow Mapping Affinity Clustering
🏥

Small Orgs: Generalist Buyers

One person handles every phase of procurement—requisition through payment—requiring a streamlined, all-in-one view.

🏢

Large Orgs: Specialized Roles

Higher transaction volume drives specialization. Each role needs a focused, task-specific interface that reduces noise.

🔄

Repetitive Task Overload

Most buyers felt overwhelmed by daily volume. They wanted the system to surface what needs action—not hunt for it.

🎯

Proactive Over Reactive

Users consistently asked for the system to tell them what to do next, rather than requiring manual status checks.

3Design Strategy & Execution

Three strategic shifts to unify the experience at a systems level

01

Persona-Driven Workflows

Tailored available features and navigation to each role's actual responsibilities. Reduced cognitive load by surfacing only what each user needs to accomplish their job—aligned to how they actually work, not how the system was architected.

Systems Thinking
02

Unified Aesthetic via Design System

Partnered with the Fireball Design System team to drive consistent visual vocabulary across all procurement modules. Collaborated with engineering to ensure implementation fidelity—not just design specs on a shelf.

Design System
03

Surface Actions Proactively

Redesigned the dashboard and task flow to prioritize work-to-be-done. Users see a prioritized action queue on arrival—eliminating the "go find it" burden that drove the most negative feedback in research.

4Outcomes & Impact

Measurable improvements in usability, consistency, and market confidence

⚡

40% Reduction in Task Steps

Primary procurement workflows were streamlined from deep, multi-click flows to focused, role-appropriate task paths—directly addressing the #1 user pain point.

🎨

Unified Visual Language via Fireball Design System

Consistent interaction patterns and visual components were implemented across all applications, eliminating the fragmented feel that hurt sales demos and user adoption.

📈

Positive Sales & Demo Reception

Sales and demo teams reported strong positive feedback from prospective clients, directly supporting the client's goal of recovering market share from ERP competitors.

🧭

Reduced Navigation Confusion

Role-driven workflows and proactive task surfacing reduced the cognitive burden of navigating complex procurement systems for users across all organization sizes.

What This Project Demonstrates

Systems-level experience design

Unified fragmented multi-product ecosystems into coherent, role-driven customer journeys.

Cross-functional design leadership

Partnered with product, engineering, and design system teams to influence roadmap and implementation quality.

Research-informed strategy

Translated qualitative insights into actionable design principles that shaped both product direction and visual systems.

End-to-end journey ownership

Managed the complete design lifecycle—discovery through high-fidelity delivery—across a complex, multi-touchpoint ecosystem.
